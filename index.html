Live Traffic Cameras

#10: August 19, 2016

Anyway, the above map shows you traffic cameras from Seattle's Department of Transportation to maybe help you avoid some of that traffic. How Many People Live Within
Walking Distance to the Lightrail?

#9: August 12, 2016

Transit expert Jarrett Walker says that the standard distance that people will walk to catch a train or subway in the US is about 400 meters (or 1/4 mile), but that could range up to a half mile for faster rail transit.

The above map shows a walking network of standard distances for how long people walk to the lightrail. Do you live in the zones? See the map for stats displaying how many people live within those zones. Click on the road segments to see how far the center of the segment is from the nearest lightrail stop. Its interesting that while 46,749 people live within a half mile of the lightrail stops in Seattle, there were 64,904 riders on average June weekdays. Also, adding stations in Capitol Hill and at the University of Washington essentially doubled ridership. So, Seattleites who travel by lightrail are likely also taking busses, carpooling in vanpools, riding bikes, and more.

Uber Destinations

#7: July 29, 2016

The above map shows the top destinations for Uber riders by neighborhoods where the rides began. Mouse over the image to see a second map, a cartogram that distorts neighborhood size based on the proportion of Uber passengers (out of all Seattle Uber passengers) starting from that neighborhood.

The data show how Uber riders gravitate towards urban centers, even if they already are close to the middle of things. Even people starting in Capitol Hill go to Capitol Hill most often. Research shows that Americans nationwide are drawn to places like Downtown and Capitol Hill because they tend to enjoy visiting cultural hubs as well as retail centers. Also, the map shows an interesting divide between North and South Seattle: Northerners more often go Downtown while Southerners tend to visit Capitol Hill.

A couple of warnings: Uber doesn't allow open access to their data, so I took the data from the source code from a map that they made in late 2012, so things are almost certainly different now. Also, Uber's map did a much better job showing the complexity of urban movement, and paints a much more realistic picture of how people move about cities, but I found that it had too many lines to make sense of what's happening, and an oversimplification was in order.

Restaurant Inspections

#6: July 22, 2016

The CDC estimates that every year 48 million people get sick and 3,000 are killed due to foodborne diseases. And, according to one study that took place in Seattle, restaurants with poor inspection results and violations of proper temperature controls were "between five and ten times more likely to have foodborne illness outbreaks."

The above map shows inspection ratings by restaurant in Seattle. I wouldn't take it to mean much if a restaurant received an "unsatisfactory" score on a couple of inspections, everybody has an off day, but repeated unsatisfactory ratings should raise some questions.

Also a quick note on ethnic restaurants. These restaurants do tend to be the source of more outbreaks. But that's because these restaurants use more fresh vegetables, uncooked fish, and a large variety of ingredients with special rules for keeping diners safe. For example, did you know that sushi rice needs a pH of 4.2 or less? Or that fried rice needs to be held at 135Â°F until consumption?

Vaccination Rates at Public Elementaries

#4: July 8, 2016


This map shows the percentages of kids with all required vaccinations in Seattle public elementary schools by attendance areas for the 2015-2016 school year. Bubble size increases with school enrollment.

For point of reference, studies say immunization rates need to from above 80% to above 95% for herd immunity to prevent spread of disease. This is, of course, vaccine dependent. Meanwhile, kids in Seattle have lower rates of vaccinations than kids in developing countries, and parents' fears of vaccines causing autism appear to be completely unfounded.

Park Features Per Capita

#3: July 1, 2016

Researchers have found that "parks with more features are more likely to be used for physical activity," and people who live near parks are likely to be more active and have healthier lifestyles. So, it appears that having a park in the neighborhood (with more features) is a good thing. How many park features per 10,000 people are in your neighborhood?

Park features include paths, tennis courts, restrooms, views, waterfront views, drinking fountains, clusters of benches,* and more.

Smith Tower According to LiDAR

#2: June 24, 2016

Did you know that Smith Tower, which was once the tallest building West of the Mississippi (and 4th tallest in the world) has an apartment unit at the top? It looks amazing. Not to be outdone by the inside, this is how the exterior of the tower looks according to LiDAR; a technology that uses space lasers to get high-res elevation datasets. LiDAR is also used to make a type of art that's reminiscent of pointillism.
