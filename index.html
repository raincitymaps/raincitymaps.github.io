Live Traffic Cameras

Traffic is a big problem in Seattle. According to one estimate, traffic costs the average Seattle commuter $1,491 per year in wasted gas and lost productivity.

Checking the above map can help you avoid some jams. It displays live images from traffic cameras from Seattle's Department of Transportation as well as a traffic overlay from Google. Click on one of the cameras to see a live picture from that traffic camera. The pictures update themselves every minute, but some cameras may be broken, so check the date/time on the image to be sure.

Uber Destinations

The above map shows the top destinations for Uber riders by neighborhoods from where the rides began. Mouse over the image to see a second map, a cartogram that distorts neighborhood size based on the proportion of Uber passengers (out of all Seattle Uber passengers) starting from that neighborhood.

The data show how Uber riders gravitate towards the urban centers, even if they're already close to the middle of things. Even people starting in Capitol Hill take Ubers to Capitol Hill most often. Research shows that Americans nationwide are drawn to places like Downtown and Capitol Hill because they tend to enjoy visiting cultural hubs as well as retail centers. Also, the map shows an interesting divide between North and South Seattle: Northerners more often go Downtown while Southerners tend to visit Capitol Hill.

A couple of warnings: Uber doesn't give open access to their data, so I took the data from the source code from a public map that they made in late 2012, so things are almost certainly different now. Also, Uber's map did a much better job showing the complexity of urban movement, and paints a much more realistic picture of how people move about cities, but I found that it had too many lines to make sense of what is happening, so I created a simpler version.

Restaurant Inspections

The CDC estimates that every year 48 million people get sick and 3,000 are killed due to foodborne diseases. And, according to a study that took place in Seattle, restaurants with poor inspection results and violations of proper temperature controls were "between five and ten times more likely to have foodborne illness outbreaks."

The above map shows inspection ratings by restaurant in Seattle. I wouldn't take it to mean much if a restaurant received an "unsatisfactory" score on a couple of inspections, but a low percentage of satisfactory ratings at a restaurant with multiple inspections might raise some questions.

Also a quick note on ethnic restaurants. These restaurants do tend to be the source of more outbreaks. But that's because these restaurants use more fresh vegetables, uncooked fish, and a larger variety of ingredients with special requirements. For example, did you know that sushi rice needs a pH of 4.2 or less? Or that fried rice needs to be held above 135°F until consumption?

Data:

But any large database will have plenty of errors. Contact me if something is inaccurate and I will fix it ASAP. Otherwise, I'm covered.

Vaccination Rates at Public Elementaries


According to immunize.org, vaccines have saved more than a billion lives in the past 50 years. Meanwhile, kids in Seattle have lower vaccination rates than kids in developing countries. A large proportion of this is because some parents are afraid that vaccines cause autism, but these fears appear to be completely unfounded.

The above map shows the percentages of kids with all required vaccinations in Seattle public elementary schools by attendance areas for the 2015-2016 school year. For point of reference, studies say immunization rates need to from above 80% to above 95% for herd immunity to prevent the spread of certain diseases. Parents, please vaccinate your kids!

Park Features Per Capita

Researchers have found that "parks with more features are more likely to be used for physical activity," and people who live near parks are likely to be more active and have healthier lifestyles. So, it appears that having a park in the neighborhood (with more features) is a good thing. How many park features per 10,000 people are in your neighborhood?

Park features include paths, tennis courts, restrooms, views, waterfront views, drinking fountains, clusters of benches,* and more.

Smith Tower According to LiDAR

Did you know that Smith Tower--which was once the tallest building West of the Mississippi (and 4th tallest in the world)--has an apartment unit at the top? It looks amazing. Not to be outdone by the inside, this is how the exterior of the tower looks according to LiDAR; a technology that uses space lasers to get high-res elevation datasets. LiDAR is also used to make a type of art reminiscent of pointillism.

Buildings with Affordable Rent

Anyway, here's a map of most* of the MFTE buildings are in Seattle.
